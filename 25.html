GTMS Car #25

Cheif Engineer

During my senior year I was voted into the position of Chief Engineer for GT Motorsports (GTMS). This was a role that I had worked for since joining the team back in Freshman year. As Chief Engineer, I had a lot of responsibilities including giving a team of 35 students technical direction.

I learnt a lot while fulfilling the role of Chief Engineer. The competition had made a big change in the roles for Aerodynamic devices (Wings, undertray etc.) which meant all teams had to go through a complete redesign. I took this opportunity to have the first car in GTMS run a full aerodynamics kit. Moreover, we wanted to cut 50lbs off of the previous years car.

At the start of the school year, I came up with overall goals for the team: a Top 10 finish in the Design event, and a Top 20 finish overall in FSAE Michigan. Then, I listed some design goals for the racecar that would help us achieve our goals. As a team, we had a chronic problem of not transferring knowledge properly from the seniors to the juniors. Along with the Team Lead, Alex, we wanted to solve this issue too.

Not only did I have to learn and teach the members (60 registered students, 35 taken to competition) the technical aspects of designing, manufacturing and testing a racecar, I also had to learn the management aspects of the position. This inluded, managing a budget of over $120,000, actively talking to sponsors, faculty and school management, and most importantly that the team did not stray from it's task of taking a well designed car to competition.

At the end of the year, we came close to our design goal: coming in 12th, but fell short of our Top 20 finish as we finished 43rd because we couldn't finish the Endurance race.
