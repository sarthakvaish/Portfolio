Sloan Automotive Lab - MIT

Research Assistant

Dr. Tian Tian's group in the Sloan Automotive Lab at MIT deals with lubrication and friction in the combustion chamber of Internal Combustion engines. The group does both simulation and experimental work. We develop simulation tools for our industry sponsors, while using the experimental side to validate and improve not only the simulations but also recommend new designs.

I work on the Floating Liner Engine, more on the experimental side. This engine was designed by Tokyo City University and can measure the friction between the piston skirt, rings and the liner. Different liners, piston skirt profiles and Oil control rings are tested to see how different paramaters effect the friction of the system. I also use the simulation tools developed by our lab to recommend various skirt profiles that will help reduce oil consumption and reduce friction.

Being one of 2 experimental students in the lab, I get to do a lot of side projects to improve and keep the engine running. This includes everything from learning about the data acquisition systems employed to changing and improving the wiring harness.
