MIT Hyperloop

Member Aerostructures Team

I joined Hyperloop in January 2016, in my second semester at MIT. As a new concept that no one has designed before, it presented an amazing learning opportunity to me.

As a member of the aerostructures team, I was charged with the responsibility to perform Finite Element Analysis on the frame of the pod. Using the loads developed by other subteams, I performed worst case braking analysis too and made sure that the frame would be able to handle these loads.

After performing FEAs I also helped with the carbon fiber shell for the pod. It had already been designed by the aerodynamist but needed to be manufactured. We did this at Whitecap Composites in Peabody. We started from MDF board which we had to CNC and stack to get the shape. Next, we had to sand it down from 40 grit to about 150 grit sandpaper. After coating it with a waterproof agent we sanded it to 1500 grit. Afterwards, we used a Resin Infusion technique which I had never done before, to layup the carbon. We made 2 shells using this process.

The competition takes part in January 2017 and we hope to present a strong pod and win the compeition.
